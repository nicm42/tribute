I'm Sorry I Haven't a Clue was created in 1972 by comedian Graeme Garden, as an 'antidote to panel games'. Four panellists played silly games, partly improvisational, for which no points were ever actually awarded. Its most famous game, Mornington Crescent, is often played on internet forums, to the bemusement of those who have never listened and are confused by the rules.

Recently they began a live tour, including the best of the games, which wasn't recorded or broadcast. Tickets to the tour and the recordings can sell out in days, if not minutes.

Mornington Crescent

The aim of the game is to reach Mornington Crescent, via other London stations. There are many variations on the rules.

One Song to the Tune of Another

A game that needs careful explanation. Often one song will be tonally opposite to the tune, for hilarious results.

Uxbridge English Dictionary

Previously known as new definitions. Using the sound of words, panellists come up with new things they could mean.

In My Pants

A new game, designed to make Jack Dee laugh. Panellists call out the name of eg a film, then Jack says "In my pants".

Swanee Kazoo

Never did a duet between two instruments sound so harmonious.

Just a Minim

Singing a song without hesitation, repetition or deviation.

Sound Charades

Charades, but telling a story in order to guess the book/TV series/film.
